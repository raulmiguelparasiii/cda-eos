# LLM assistance log

## Scope

Large language models were used as drafting, algebra-structuring, coding, debugging, and numerical-workflow assistants during the development of this framework paper and prototype.

LLMs were used for:

- drafting and restructuring prose,
- proposing alternative notation and figure/caption organization,
- checking algebraic consistency at the level of symbolic manipulation,
- designing numerical proof-of-concept workflows,
- writing and debugging Python prototype code,
- interpreting preliminary numerical outputs generated by the author in Colab.

LLMs were not used as a substitute for experimental measurement, peer review, or final scientific authority.

## Tools

- OpenAI ChatGPT GPT-5.1 and GPT-5.5 Thinking, accessed through the ChatGPT UI.
- xAI Grok models, used interactively for brainstorming and literature-oriented questioning.
- Google Colab, used by the author to run CoolProp-based numerical prototype calculations.

## Numerical work

The preliminary numerical prototype uses CoolProp as a practical direct-property backend for real water fields. The prototype includes base-field stability checks, a direct-metric curvature route in \((T,v_m)\), a curvature-to-network-factor map, a pressure-level association correction sweep, and a prototype heat-capacity consistency check.

The paper does not present this as a final fitted reference EOS. It presents CDA-EOS as a reproducible curvature-feedback EOS construction with preliminary consistency checks.
